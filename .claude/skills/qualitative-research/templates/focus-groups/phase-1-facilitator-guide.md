## Discussion Guide

**Purpose:** Structured agenda to guide discussion while allowing organic interaction.

### Session Structure

**Total time:** [90-120 minutes]

1. **Welcome & Setup** (10 min)
2. **Warm-up** (10 min)
3. **Core Discussion** (50-70 min)
4. **Wrap-up & Closing** (10-20 min)

---

### 1. Welcome & Setup (10 minutes)

**Welcome participants:**
"Thank you all for joining today's discussion on [topic]. I'm [name] and I'll be facilitating our conversation."

**Purpose & goals:**
"We're here to understand [research goal]. Your experiences and perspectives are valuable. There are no right or wrong answers - we want to hear diverse viewpoints."

**Consent & recording:**
"This session will be recorded [audio/video] so I can focus on facilitating rather than taking notes. The recording will be transcribed and [stored securely / deleted after transcription]. Your comments will be [anonymous / confidential] in any reports."

**Obtain consent:** [Verbal on recording / Written consent forms]

**Ground rules:**
1. "One person speaks at a time"
2. "All perspectives are welcome - there's no right answer"
3. "What's said here stays here (confidentiality)"
4. "It's OK to disagree - respectfully"
5. "Please silence phones and minimize distractions"
6. "[Virtual only] Keep video on if possible, use chat for questions"

**Introductions:**
"Let's go around and introduce ourselves. Please share [name, role/context, and how long you've been working with topic]."

---

### 2. Warm-Up (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Build rapport, get people talking in low-stakes way.

**Warm-up question:**
"To start, let's go around and everyone share: [easy, relevant question]"

**Examples:**
- "What's your first memory of encountering [topic]?"
- "If you had to describe [topic] in three words, what would they be?"
- "What brought you to [industry/role/situation]?"

**Facilitator notes:**
- Keep this light and quick
- Ensure everyone speaks (sets pattern for participation)
- Listen for themes to explore later

---

### 3. Core Discussion (50-70 minutes)

**CRITICAL:** These are TOPIC prompts, not rigid scripts. Follow the conversation.

#### Topic 1: Current Experience (15-20 min)

**Opening prompt:**
"Let's start by talking about how you currently [handle situation / use product / experience topic]. Can someone share how this works for you?"

**Follow-up probes:**
- "How long have you been doing it this way?"
- "Does anyone have a different experience?"
- "What's working well about this approach?"
- "What's challenging or frustrating?"

**Facilitator goals:**
- Get 3-4 people to share specific examples
- Encourage others to build on or contrast with what's been shared
- Note areas of consensus and disagreement

---

#### Topic 2: Specific Challenges (15-20 min)

**Opening prompt:**
"I'd like to hear about times when [topic] has been particularly challenging. Can someone describe a specific situation?"

**Follow-up probes:**
- "What made that situation difficult?"
- "Has anyone else experienced something similar?"
- "What did you do in that situation?"
- "What would have made it better?"

**Facilitator goals:**
- Get concrete stories, not vague complaints
- Explore the CONTEXT of challenges
- Understand what they've tried (reveals actual behavior vs hypotheticals)

---

#### Topic 3: Solutions & Attempts (15-20 min)

**Opening prompt:**
"What have you tried to address [challenge/problem] we've been discussing?"

**Follow-up probes:**
- "Why did you choose that approach?"
- "What happened when you tried it?"
- "Why did you stick with it / stop using it?"
- "What were you comparing it against?"

**Facilitator goals:**
- Understand past behavior (Mom Test principle)
- Identify what they've actually spent time/money on
- Reveal decision-making criteria

---

#### Topic 4: Ideal State (10-15 min)

**Opening prompt:**
"If you could wave a magic wand and change [topic], what would be different?"

**Follow-up probes:**
- "Why would that matter to you?"
- "How would that change your workflow/experience?"
- "What would you be willing to trade for that?"

**Facilitator notes:**
- This is somewhat hypothetical (use sparingly)
- Focus on the "why" behind what they want
- Connect back to real challenges discussed earlier

---

### 4. Wrap-Up & Closing (10-20 minutes)

**Summary:**
"Before we close, let me summarize what I've heard... [Summarize 3-4 key themes]. Did I capture that accurately? Anything I missed?"

**Final question (ALWAYS ask):**
"Is there anything about [topic] that we haven't discussed that you think is important?"

**Thank you:**
"Thank you all for sharing your time and perspectives. Your insights are incredibly valuable."

## Facilitator Techniques

### Managing Group Dynamics

#### The Dominant Talker

**Symptoms:** One person answers every question, talks over others

**Interventions:**
1. **Direct others:** "Thank you [Name]. I'd love to hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet. [Other name], what's your experience?"
2. **Use hand raising:** "Let's hear from a few different people. Raise your hand if you've experienced this."
3. **Private check-in:** [If virtual] Private message: "Thank you for your input. Can you help me draw out quieter voices?"

#### The Quiet Participant

**Symptoms:** Someone hasn't spoken much, seems disengaged

**Interventions:**
1. **Direct invitation:** "[Name], I notice you haven't shared yet - I'd love to hear your perspective on this."
2. **Easier entry point:** Ask them to respond to something specific vs open-ended question
3. **Non-verbal check:** [If in-person] Eye contact, open body language to invite them in
4. **Accept silence:** Some people process by listening - that's OK

#### The Tangent

**Symptoms:** Discussion veers off-topic

**Interventions:**
1. **Acknowledge & redirect:** "That's interesting. Let's bookmark that and come back to [topic]."
2. **Check relevance:** "How does this relate to [topic]?"
3. **Group decision:** "This is interesting - should we explore this or return to [topic]?"

#### The Disagreement

**Symptoms:** Participants disagree, tension rises

**This is GOOD - lean into it:**
1. **Normalize:** "It sounds like there are different experiences here - that's valuable."
2. **Explore:** "Tell me more about why you see it differently."
3. **Avoid resolving:** Don't try to create consensus - diversity is the goal
4. **Intervene only if:** Disrespectful or personal attacks

#### The Groupthink

**Symptoms:** Everyone agrees quickly, little depth

**Interventions:**
1. **Push deeper:** "What makes you say that? Can you give a specific example?"
2. **Invite dissent:** "Does anyone see this differently?"
3. **Play devil's advocate:** "Some people might say [contrarian view] - what do you think?"

---

### Probing Questions

**When someone gives surface-level answer:**
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "Tell me more about that"
- "What did that look like in practice?"

**When someone states opinion without context:**
- "Why do you think that?"
- "What experience led you to that conclusion?"

**When you want to explore difference:**
- "How is that different from [earlier point]?"
- "Does anyone have a contrasting experience?"

**When you want depth:**
- "Walk me through what happened step by step"
- "What was the outcome?"
- "How did that make you feel?"

- [ ] Provide dial-in number as backup
- [ ] Have co-facilitator or note-taker to monitor chat

- "Mute when not speaking if there's background noise"
- "Use chat for questions or if you're having trouble breaking in"
- "Use 'raise hand' feature if helpful"

---

### Roles & Responsibilities

**Facilitator (you):**
- Guide discussion using discussion guide
- Ensure everyone participates
- Manage time
- Probe for depth
- Maintain neutral, curious stance

**Note-taker (optional but recommended):**
- Document who said what (pseudonyms)
- Note non-verbal cues (body language, tone)
- Track themes emerging
- Flag moments to revisit
- Monitor time

**Tech support (for virtual):**
- Manage recording
- Monitor chat
- Handle tech issues
- Admit late arrivals
